1. **Consider the Context of Comorbidities**: Evaluate the overall health context of the patient. Multiple serious conditions may obscure the presence of lipid disorders, so consider the interplay of diagnoses rather than relying solely on isolated findings.

2. **Evaluate Medication Prescriptions in Combination**: Look for combinations of lipid-lowering medications (e.g., statins) with other relevant diagnoses (e.g., diabetes, hypertension) that may indicate a higher likelihood of lipid disorders, even if no explicit diagnosis is mentioned.

3. **Assess the Significance of Hyperlipidemia Diagnoses**: A diagnosis of hyperlipidemia (e.g., unspecified hyperlipidemia, pure hypercholesterolemia) should be weighted heavily, especially when accompanied by lipid-lowering medications, as these indicate active management of lipid metabolism.

4. **Identify Patterns of Evidence**: Recognize that the presence of multiple indicators (e.g., a diagnosis of hyperlipidemia, prescription of a statin, and related comorbidities) can collectively support a positive prediction, even if each individual piece of evidence is not definitive on its own.
