1. Documented prescription of lipid-lowering agents (e.g., Atorvastatin, Pravastatin, Simvastatin) strongly supports a positive prediction.
2. Explicit diagnosis of a primary lipid disorder (e.g., Pure hypercholesterolemia) is a highly reliable indicator for a positive prediction.
3. The combination of cardiovascular diagnoses (e.g., Coronary atherosclerosis, Heart failure) alongside documented lipid-lowering therapy suggests a positive prediction.
4. The presence of metabolic risk factors (e.g., Diabetes, Hypertension) combined with documented cardiovascular issues warrants a positive prediction.
5. Documented prescription of multiple cardiovascular medications (e.g., Metoprolol, Lisinopril) in a patient with metabolic risk factors should be considered supportive.
6. The combination of cardiovascular disease and metabolic risk factors, even without explicit lipid-lowering medication, should be considered suggestive if other risk factors are present.
7. Explicit diagnosis of a primary lipid disorder is sufficient evidence for a positive prediction, regardless of other documented signals.
8. The presence of multiple cardiovascular and metabolic diagnoses, even without explicit lipid-lowering medication, should be considered suggestive if other risk factors are present.
