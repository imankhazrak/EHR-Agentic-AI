1. **Evaluate Comorbidities Holistically**: Assess the interplay of multiple diagnoses (e.g., diabetes, hypertension, obesity) to identify higher risks for complications, rather than relying on single diagnoses.

2. **Prioritize Strong Clinical Indicators**: Focus on well-established clinical indicators (e.g., uncontrolled diabetes, recent hospitalizations) that correlate directly with predicted outcomes, avoiding reliance on weak or isolated signals.

3. **Analyze Medication Profiles**: Use prescribed medications as predictive tools, recognizing that certain medications (e.g., statins for hyperlipidemia, antihypertensives for cardiovascular risk) can indicate underlying conditions.

4. **Identify Risk Factor Combinations**: Evaluate combinations of risk factors (e.g., obesity, diabetes, hypertension) collectively to strengthen predictions for related complications.

5. **Avoid Rigid Diagnostic Criteria**: Allow for nuanced predictions by considering risk factors and clinical indicators without requiring explicit diagnoses for all positive predictions.

6. **Recognize Patterns of Complications**: Use known associations between conditions (e.g., diabetes leading to cardiovascular disease) to inform predictions, especially when multiple related conditions are present.

7. **Prioritize Recent Clinical Events**: Give more weight to recent health events (e.g., hospital visits, procedures)
