1. **Evaluate Comorbidities Holistically**: Consider the interplay of multiple diagnoses (e.g., diabetes, hypertension, obesity) when predicting outcomes. A combination of these conditions should strengthen the prediction for related complications, rather than relying on single diagnoses.

2. **Prioritize Strong Clinical Indicators**: Focus on well-established clinical indicators (e.g., uncontrolled diabetes, acute kidney failure) that have a direct correlation with the predicted outcomes. Avoid over-relying on weak signals or isolated findings that do not provide sufficient evidence for a positive prediction.

3. **Assess Medication Relevance**: Analyze prescribed medications for their relevance to the predicted conditions. For instance, the presence of statins (e.g., atorvastatin) should be considered a strong indicator for lipid disorders, while medications for hypertension should support predictions for cardiovascular disease.

4. **Identify Risk Factor Combinations**: Recognize that the presence of multiple risk factors (e.g., obesity, diabetes, hypertension) can collectively indicate a higher likelihood of complications. Ensure that combinations of risk factors are evaluated together to support positive predictions.
